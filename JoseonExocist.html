What swords did Joseon people use?

During the Joseon dynasty, swords were in production both for military and ceremonial use. Several types of ceremonial swords were made; among these sword types are the jingeom (dragon sword) and ingeom (tiger sword), which by tradition could be forged only at certain times. The highest grade of these, sa-ingeom (four tigers sword) and possibly the sa-jingeom (four dragons sword - none are extant) were reserved for the monarch and could only be made during a window of 2 hours every 12 years. The swords that Joseon soldiers used were crafted with the greatest care, using only high quality steel considered for the use of military swords.

What is gat, Korean traditional hat?

A gat is the name of Korean traditional hat worn by men along with hanbok (Korean traditional clothing) during the Joseon period. It is made from horsehair with a bamboo frame and is partly transparent. Most gat are cylindrical in shape with a wide brim on a bamboo frame. Before the late 19th century, only noble class men could wear gat, which represented their social status and protected their topknots (sangtu). It has been designated as Intangible Cultural Property No. 4 on December 24, 1964.

How was the food culture in Joseon Dynasty?

The Joseon Dynasty was one of the most influential time periods in Korea. Many staples of Korean cuisine were also being developed at this time. Meals included side dishes such as mandu, noodles, rice cakes, porridge, soups, and pickled vegetables. Meals were elaborate if you were at the top, there was a specific way you would place dishes and the amount you would get depended on your rank. The king would get twelve dishes and nobility would get around nine.
